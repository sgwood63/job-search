# Base Documents

This directory contains your master resume and cover letter - the foundation for all customized applications.

## What Goes Here

### Master Resume
Your comprehensive resume with:
- All relevant experience
- Full range of skills
- Key achievements
- Complete project history

**File naming**: `resume-master.pdf` (and source file like `.docx`, `.pages`, etc.)

### Master Cover Letter
A flexible cover letter template with:
- Your authentic voice established
- Modular structure for easy customization
- Placeholders for company-specific details

**File naming**: `cover-letter-master.pdf` (and source file)

## Best Practices

### For Your Master Resume

1. **Keep it comprehensive**: Include everything relevant, even if the master is longer than what you'd submit
2. **Organize clearly**: Easy to scan and pull from when customizing
3. **Use strong action verbs**: Make it easy to copy strong bullets
4. **Include metrics**: Quantify achievements where possible
5. **Keep it updated**: Add new accomplishments as they happen

### For Your Master Cover Letter

1. **Establish your voice**: This should sound like you
2. **Modular paragraphs**: Each paragraph should be semi-independent
3. **Use placeholders**: `[Company]`, `[Role]`, `[Specific detail]` for easy customization
4. **Multiple versions OK**: You might have 2-3 base versions for different profile types

## Source Files

Keep both source files (editable) and PDFs:
- Source: `.docx`, `.pages`, `.tex`, etc. - for making customizations
- PDF: Final formatted version

## Customization Workflow

**Do NOT edit these files directly for applications**

Instead:
1. Copy the relevant base document to your application folder
2. Customize the copy for that specific role
3. Keep these master versions as your single source of truth

## Maintenance

**Update your master documents**:
- After completing a major project
- When you develop a new skill
- When you find a particularly strong way to phrase something
- Monthly review to keep them fresh

**Version control**:
- Consider keeping dated backups (e.g., `resume-master-2026-02.pdf`)
- Or use git to track changes over time

## Getting Started

If you haven't added your documents yet:

1. **Start with what you have**: Copy your current resume and cover letter here
2. **Polish them**: This is your chance to refine without time pressure
3. **Get feedback**: Consider having someone review for voice and clarity
4. **Establish your baseline**: These become your quality standard

Your master documents should represent your best, most authentic professional self.
