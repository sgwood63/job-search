For any JD where the end company is not explicitly named (recruiter postings, stealth, "confidential client"), always research to identify the likely end company before or during document generation.

**Why:** Knowing the actual company enables better resume tailoring, smarter interview prep, and surfaces useful intel (stage, funding, customers, product positioning) that generic JD language obscures. The RediRecruit/Unify case showed this is highly actionable — an 85–90% confident ID from public sources in a single research pass.

**How to apply:**
- Run a research agent using clues from the JD: product description phrases, location, comp range, recruiter name, industry focus, stage signals
- Cross-reference against job boards (Built In, Ashby, Lever, Greenhouse) for exact comp/location matches
- Check if recruiter firm has any public client disclosures
- Look for near word-for-word language matches between JD and company marketing copy
- Rank candidates by confidence; include in job-description.md under "Company Research" section and in notes.md under "Company Context"
- Add action item to confirm identity on first recruiter call
- Update tracker entry to reflect likely company name (with "likely" qualifier)
