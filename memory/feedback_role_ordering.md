**Definition: Old Roles**
An old role is one that ended over 10 years ago. ie. if we are in 2026, an old role is one that ended before 2016.

**Rule 1: Strict reverse chronological order**
All roles included in a resume must appear most-recent-first. Skipping roles is acceptable; displaying them out of order is not.

**Rule 2: Section heading is "Relevant Experience"**
The experience section must always be headed `## RELEVANT EXPERIENCE`, not `## EXPERIENCE` or `## PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE`.

**Rule 3: Earlier Career is a subsection under Relevant Experience**
All old roles must be grouped under an `### Earlier Career` (or `## EARLIER CAREER`) sub-heading.  Within Earlier Career, also maintain reverse chronological order.

**Rule 4: Earlier Career roles are in reverse order within the subsection**

**Why:** Consistent structure makes documents scannable and prevents timeline confusion. Old role details are to be appropriately compressed into Earlier Career without cluttering the main section.

**How to apply:**
- Use `## RELEVANT EXPERIENCE` always
- Add `### Earlier Career` or `## EARLIER CAREER` before any old roles
- Never place an old role in the main section above a role that is not old
