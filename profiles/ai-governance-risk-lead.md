## Core Value Proposition

Senior AI professional who operationalizes AI governance, risk management, and responsible AI frameworks in enterprise and regulated environments. Bridges technical AI implementation with regulatory compliance, risk mitigation, and ethical AI practices.

Deep understanding of AI systems combined with governance frameworks, regulatory requirements (NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, SOC 2, GDPR), and enterprise risk management. Can work cross-functionally with data science, engineering, legal, security, and compliance teams while providing trusted advisory to senior leadership.

Age and seniority are significant assets - this role requires judgment, credibility with executives, and "seen it before" resilience that comes with experience.

## Key Experience Areas

1. **AI Governance Frameworks & Implementation**:
   - Model risk management and validation
   - Bias detection and mitigation strategies
   - Explainability and transparency frameworks
   - Data privacy and security in AI systems
   - Model lifecycle management, monitoring, auditability

2. **Regulatory Compliance & Standards**:
   - NIST AI Risk Management Framework
   - EU AI Act compliance
   - SOC 2, GDPR, emerging US AI regulations
   - Industry-specific regulations (FCRA, ECOA for financial services)
   - AI policy development and implementation

3. **Enterprise AI Risk Management**:
   - AI risk assessment and classification
   - Risk mitigation strategies
   - Escalation management
   - Compliance reporting to executives and board
   - Third-party AI risk (vendor management, AI SaaS tools)

4. **Cross-Functional Leadership**:
   - Working with data science, MLOps, engineering teams
   - Partnering with legal, security, compliance functions
   - Trusted advisor to C-level (CIO, CISO, CRO, Chief AI Officer)
   - Stakeholder management across technical and non-technical audiences

## Technical Skills & Tools

- **AI/ML Understanding**: Model architectures, training pipelines, deployment patterns
- **Governance Tools**: Model monitoring, bias detection, explainability tools
- **Risk Frameworks**: NIST AI RMF, ISO standards, industry frameworks
- **Data Governance**: Data lineage, metadata management, data quality
- **Cloud Platforms**: AI governance in AWS, Azure, GCP environments
- **Documentation**: Policy writing, framework documentation, audit trails

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Pure legal/compliance roles without technical component
- "AI ethics" roles that are primarily philosophical vs. operational
- Roles with no budget or organizational authority
- "Innovation theater" - governance in name only
- Roles reporting too low in organization (needs C-level visibility)

## Interview Prep Notes

**Key talking points:**
1. Framework for operationalizing AI governance
2. Example of model risk assessment and mitigation
3. Regulatory compliance implementation (specific regulation)
4. Cross-functional stakeholder management
5. Board-level reporting on AI risk
6. Balancing innovation with risk management

**Questions to ask:**
- What's driving the need for AI governance now? (regulatory, risk events, proactive)
- How is the AI governance function structured? (Centralized, federated, COE)
- What's the relationship with existing risk management functions?
- How mature is the organization's AI deployment?
- What are the biggest AI risk concerns right now?
- What does success look like in first 6-12 months?
- Budget and resources for this function?
