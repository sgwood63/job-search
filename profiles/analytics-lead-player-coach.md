# Job Profile: Analytics Lead / Head of Analytics (Player-Coach)

*Created: 2026-02-16*

## Profile Overview

**Target Role Types**:
- Analytics Lead (IC or small team)
- Head of Analytics (hands-on)
- Principal Data Scientist (analytics flavor)
- Director of Analytics (player-coach, NOT large team)
- Analytics Architect
- Advanced Analytics Lead

**Industries/Sectors**:
- Mid-size enterprises (500-2,000 employees) - need senior person but can't afford large team
- Financial services, insurance, healthcare
- Retail, e-commerce
- B2B SaaS companies
- Manufacturing with analytics focus

**Company Size Preference**:
- Sweet spot: 500-2,000 employees
- Enterprises: 2,000+ if role is IC or small matrixed team
- Avoid: Early-stage startups (too much building from scratch)

## Core Value Proposition

Senior analytics professional who delivers insights AND influences how they're used. Combines hands-on analytical work with strategic guidance, stakeholder management, and setting standards. Not pure engineering, not pure management - the "player-coach" who can do the work and guide others.

Brings architectural thinking to analytics: how to build sustainable, scalable analytics capabilities, not just one-off analyses. Can work across business functions, understands when to build vs. buy, and knows how to make analytics actually drive decisions (not just produce dashboards).

Age and seniority are significant assets - companies want judgment, business acumen, and someone who won't get lost in technical rabbit holes. "Analytics that drive action" > "perfect models nobody uses."

## Key Experience Areas

1. **Hands-On Analytics Work**:
   - Business analytics and reporting
   - Predictive analytics and modeling (when appropriate)
   - Dashboard and visualization design
   - SQL, Python, analytics tools
   - Data exploration and insight generation

2. **Analytics Architecture & Standards**:
   - Analytics platform selection and implementation
   - Data modeling and semantic layers
   - Governance and quality standards
   - Reusable assets and templates
   - Documentation and best practices

3. **Stakeholder Management & Influence**:
   - Working with executives and business leaders
   - Translating business questions into analytics approach
   - Presenting insights and driving action
   - Influencing decision-making with data
   - Managing expectations and prioritization

4. **Team Leadership (Light Touch)**:
   - Mentoring analysts or data scientists (small team or matrixed)
   - Setting direction and standards
   - Code review and quality assurance
   - Hiring and assessing talent
   - NOT managing 10+ people or dealing with HR overhead

## Technical Skills & Tools

- **Analytics & BI**: Pyramid Analytics, Jaspersoft JasperReports Server, TIBCO Spotfire (platforms competitive with Tableau/Power BI), dashboard development, reporting, data visualization
- **Data**: SQL (expert level - extensive experience with data extraction, transformation, integration), data modeling, data warehousing concepts
- **Programming**: Python for analysis (pandas, scikit-learn basics)
- **Statistical**: A/B testing, forecasting, segmentation, basic ML
- **AI/ML (Applied)**: GenAI for analytics (LLMs for insights, automated analysis)
- **Cloud**: Understanding of cloud data platforms (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift), AWS
- **Data Integration**: Data pipelines, ETL, enterprise data integration

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Roles expecting you to manage 10+ person teams (unless you want that)
- "Analytics Lead" that's actually junior analyst position
- Positions focused on very specific vertical domain you don't know
- Roles that are 90% engineering (ETL, data pipelines) with no business analytics
- Organizations where "analytics" means only dashboards/reporting
- No executive access or influence - just producing reports

## Interview Prep Notes

**Key talking points:**
1. Example of analytics insight that drove significant business decision
2. Approach to building vs. buying analytics capabilities
3. How you balance hands-on work with strategic guidance
4. Working with executives who are skeptical of data
5. Analytics architecture and platform decisions
6. Mentoring approach (if relevant)
7. Making analytics accessible and actionable

**Questions to ask:**
- What are the biggest business questions analytics needs to answer?
- How is the analytics function structured? (centralized, embedded, COE)
- What's the current analytics maturity? (tooling, practices, culture)
- What does success look like in first 6 months? First year?
- Who are the key stakeholders? Level of executive access?
- Is this role more hands-on or more leadership/strategy?
- What's the data infrastructure? (warehouse, quality, governance)
- Team structure: IC, small team, matrixed influence?
- What analytics tools and platforms are currently in use?
- How are analytics priorities set?

**Technical depth questions:**
- What BI tools are used?
- What's the data warehouse/platform?
- What's the data quality and governance maturity?
- What types of analytics are most valued? (descriptive, predictive, prescriptive)
- How is AI/ML currently used, if at all?
